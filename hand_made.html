Module 4: Darkroom Method
Explore the basics of dark room method used in manufacturing PCB boards

Lamination

Lamination in PCB design is the process of bonding multiple layers of a PCB together using heat and pressure. Key characteristics include:

  • Enhances structural integrity
  • Provides electric insulation
  • Ensures precise layer alignment
  • Improves thermal and mechanical stability

Film matching

Film matching in PCB design is the process of aligning photomask films accurately to ensure proper layer registration. Its key features include:

  • Prevents misalignment defects
  • Ensures accurate circuit pattern transfer
  • Improves yield and reliability
  • Essential to multilayer PCB fabrication

UV light projection

UV light projection in PCB design is the process of using ultraviolet light to transfer circuit patterns onto a photosensitive layer. Its key feautured include:

  • Ensures precise pattern exposure
  • Enables high resolution imaging
  • Used in photoresist developement
  • Critical for fine patch in PCB manufacturing.
